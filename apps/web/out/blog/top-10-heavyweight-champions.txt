17:Tf64,<h1>Top 10 Greatest Heavyweight Champions of All Time</h1>
<p>The heavyweight division has always been the glamour division in boxing. These giants of the ring have captivated audiences for over a century. Today, we're counting down the top 10 greatest heavyweight champions in boxing history.</p>
<h2>10. Jack Dempsey (1919-1926)</h2>
<p><strong>Record:</strong> 66-6-11 (51 KOs)</p>
<p>The "Manassa Mauler" was one of boxing's first superstars. His aggressive style and knockout power made him a cultural icon in the 1920s. His fights regularly drew crowds of over 100,000 spectators.</p>
<h2>9. Evander Holyfield (1990-1992, 1993-1994, 1996-1999, 2000-2001)</h2>
<p><strong>Record:</strong> 44-10-2 (29 KOs)</p>
<p>The only four-time heavyweight champion in history. Holyfield's warrior spirit and ability to overcome adversity made him one of the most respected champions ever.</p>
<h2>8. Larry Holmes (1978-1985)</h2>
<p><strong>Record:</strong> 69-6 (44 KOs)</p>
<p>The "Easton Assassin" had one of the best jabs in heavyweight history. He made 20 successful title defenses and went 48-0 before his first loss.</p>
<h2>7. George Foreman (1973-1974, 1994-1997)</h2>
<p><strong>Record:</strong> 76-5 (68 KOs)</p>
<p>Big George had two distinct careers - a fearsome destroyer in the 1970s and an unlikely comeback king in the 1990s. At 45, he became the oldest heavyweight champion ever.</p>
<h2>6. Jack Johnson (1908-1915)</h2>
<p><strong>Record:</strong> 73-13-10 (40 KOs)</p>
<p>The first African American heavyweight champion broke barriers and defied conventions. His technical brilliance was decades ahead of his time.</p>
<h2>5. Lennox Lewis (1993-1994, 1997-2001, 2001-2004)</h2>
<p><strong>Record:</strong> 41-2-1 (32 KOs)</p>
<p>The last undisputed heavyweight champion (until recently). Lewis defeated every man he faced and avenged both his losses.</p>
<h2>4. Joe Frazier (1970-1973)</h2>
<p><strong>Record:</strong> 32-4-1 (27 KOs)</p>
<p>"Smokin' Joe" was relentless pressure personified. His left hook was legendary, and his trilogy with Ali produced some of boxing's greatest moments.</p>
<h2>3. Rocky Marciano (1952-1956)</h2>
<p><strong>Record:</strong> 49-0 (43 KOs)</p>
<p>The only heavyweight champion to retire undefeated. Marciano's incredible conditioning and punching power made him unstoppable during his era.</p>
<h2>2. Joe Louis (1937-1949)</h2>
<p><strong>Record:</strong> 66-3 (52 KOs)</p>
<p>The "Brown Bomber" held the title for a record 12 years and made 25 successful defenses. He was a hero to millions during World War II.</p>
<h2>1. Muhammad Ali (1964-1967, 1974-1978, 1978-1979)</h2>
<p><strong>Record:</strong> 56-5 (37 KOs)</p>
<p>"The Greatest" transcended boxing to become a global icon. His speed, skill, and charisma revolutionized the heavyweight division. Ali's battles with Frazier, Foreman, and others defined an era.</p>
<h2>Honorable Mentions</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Mike Tyson</strong> - The youngest heavyweight champion at 20</li>
<li><strong>Sonny Liston</strong> - One of the most feared punchers ever</li>
<li><strong>Floyd Patterson</strong> - First man to regain the heavyweight title</li>
<li><strong>Vitali Klitschko</strong> - Dominated the 2000s alongside his brother</li>
<li><strong>Wladimir Klitschko</strong> - Second-longest combined title reign in history</li>
</ul>
<h2>The Debate Continues</h2>
<p>Rankings like these always spark debate, and that's part of what makes boxing great. Each era had its dominant champions, and comparing across generations is always challenging.</p>
