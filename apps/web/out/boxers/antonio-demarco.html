Biography

Antonio DeMarco, also known as "Tony," is a talented professional boxer who was born on January 7, 1986. With a height of 178cm and a reach of 180cm, DeMarco possesses the physical attributes that make him a formidable opponent inside the ring. As a southpaw, he has the advantage of being a left-handed fighter, which often catches his opponents off guard. Fighting in the welterweight weight class, DeMarco has established himself as a force to be reckoned with in the boxing world.

DeMarco has an impressive boxing resume, with numerous victories that have solidified his place among the top fighters in his weight class. He has faced tough competition throughout his career, showcasing his skills and determination in the ring. Known for his aggressive style and ability to land powerful punches, DeMarco has become a fan favorite for his exciting and action-packed fights.

One of the defining moments in DeMarco's career came in 2011 when he captured the WBC Lightweight World Championship. In a thrilling fight against Jorge Linares, DeMarco demonstrated his resilience and punching power by knocking out his opponent in the eleventh round.
