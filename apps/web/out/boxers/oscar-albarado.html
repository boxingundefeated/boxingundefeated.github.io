Biography

Oscar Albarado, also known by his nickname "Shotgun," is a former professional boxer who made his mark in the ring during the 1970s. Born on September 15, 1948, Albarado hails from Uvalde, Texas. Standing at a height of 169cm and boasting an impressive reach of 173cm, Albarado was known for his quick hands, relentless style, and fighting spirit.

Albarado's boxing career spanned from 1970 to 1978, during which he faced off against some of the toughest opponents of his era. He competed primarily in the featherweight and lightweight divisions, showcasing his skills and determination to the boxing world.

Throughout his career, Albarado amassed an impressive professional record, with 39 wins, 7 losses, and 1 draw. Among his victories, he scored 26 knockouts, demonstrating his power inside the ring. Albarado's aggressive style made him a fan favorite, as he always brought excitement and intensity to his fights.

One of Albarado's most memorable victories came in 1974 when he defeated the highly regarded Ernesto Marcel, capturing the NABF Featherweight title. Albarado's relentless pressure and ability to withstand punishment were on full display in this fight, earning him recognition as a top contender in his weight class.
