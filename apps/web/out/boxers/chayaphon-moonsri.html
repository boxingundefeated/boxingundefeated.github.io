Biography

Chayaphon Moonsri, also known by his nicknames "Wanheng Menayothin" and "Kaiyanghada," is a highly accomplished Thai boxer in the minimumweight division. Born on October 27, 1985, Moonsri has made a significant impact in the world of boxing with his exceptional skills and determination.

Standing at a height of 158cm with a reach of 164cm, Moonsri possesses a compact frame that allows him to apply effective strategies inside the ring. As an orthodox boxer, he can exploit his opponent's weaknesses and deliver powerful punches with precision.

Weighing at an impressive 47.63kg, Moonsri competes in the minimumweight weight class. Despite his lean physique, he has proven time and time again that size is not the sole determinant of success in boxing. Moonsri's agility, speed, and technical expertise have helped him conquer opponents who may possess larger physical attributes.

Moonsri's journey in professional boxing has been nothing short of remarkable. Since making his professional debut in 2007, he has amassed an impressive undefeated record, with victories against highly skilled opponents from around the world. His dedication and relentless pursuit of excellence have earned him the recognition and respect of the boxing community.

One of the most notable achievements of Moonsri's career came on May 31, 2019, when he surpassed Floyd Mayweather's unbeaten record of 50-0 by securing his 51st consecutive victory. This remarkable feat demonstrated Moonsri's ability to consistently perform at a high level and maintain his focus in the face of adversity.

He possesses exceptional footwork, allowing him to move swiftly around the ring and create angles to unleash devastating combinations. His defensive skills are also commendable, as he relies on his quick reflexes and evasive maneuvers to avoid incoming punches.

Despite his impressive resume, Moonsri remains humble and grounded. He is known for his calm and composed demeanor both inside and outside of the ring. Moonsri understands the importance of discipline and hard work, which have undoubtedly contributed to his success.
