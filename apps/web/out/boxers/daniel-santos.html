Biography

Boxing fans and enthusiasts have witnessed countless fighters make their mark in the ring throughout the years, but only a select few have truly left an indelible impression. One such boxer is the talented Daniel Santos. Born on October 10, 1975, Santos hails from Puerto Rico and has etched his name in the annals of boxing history. Standing at an impressive height of 182cm, with a reach of 74 inches, Santos possesses the physical attributes that have helped him excel in the ring.

One notable aspect of Santos' boxing style is his southpaw stance. This means that he leads with his right hand and places his right foot forward, giving him a unique advantage over his orthodox opponents. The southpaw stance often catches opponents off-guard, as they are more accustomed to facing fighters who favor the orthodox stance, leading with their left hand and foot. Santos' southpaw stance allows him to launch powerful and unexpected punches, contributing to his success inside the ropes.

Santos has competed in two weight classes throughout his career, namely welterweight and super welterweight. As a welterweight, he competes at a weight of 61.23kg. His ability to control his weight and perform at a high level in this weight class is a testament to his dedication and disciplined approach to the sport. Moving up to super welterweight, Santos has displayed his versatility by transitioning seamlessly and competing with the best in this weight class.

Now, let's delve into Santos' boxing career and some of his most memorable moments in the ring. From the early stages of his career, Santos showed tremendous promise, building an impressive record and captivating audiences with his skill and tenacity. As he gained experience and rose through the ranks, he earned the opportunity to fight for a world title.

On August 7, 2001, Santos faced Yuri Foreman for the vacant WBO light middleweight title. This fight proved to be a defining moment in Santos' career, as he claimed victory via a 12-round unanimous decision. Santos' relentless aggression and tactical prowess allowed him to showcase his skills and secure the prestigious world title, thus marking the beginning of his championship reign.

Throughout his career, Santos faced off against notable opponents, leaving fans eagerly anticipating each bout. One such memorable fight occurred on October 6, 2007, when Santos defended his WBA super welterweight title against Joachim Alcine. The clash saw Santos exhibit his fighting spirit and determination, resulting in a hard-fought victory via split decision.

Santos' championship reign as the WBO light middleweight titleholder is particularly impressive. From March 2, 2002, to November 12, 2005, Santos successfully defended his belt against various contenders, showcasing his dominance and solidifying his status as one of the top fighters in his weight class.
