Biography

Daniel "El Raton" Zaragoza, born on December 11, 1957, is a Mexican boxing legend known for his impressive skills in the bantamweight and super bantamweight divisions. With a reach of 173 cm and a southpaw stance, Zaragoza's unique style and tenacity inside the ring made him a force to be reckoned with during his active years.

Throughout his career, Zaragoza carved out a reputation as a formidable opponent, displaying a blend of technical proficiency, power, and durability that earned him the respect of fans and fellow boxers alike. His ability to adapt his strategy to different opponents and his unrivaled determination made him a true champion.

Competing in the bantamweight and super bantamweight weight classes, Zaragoza faced off against some of the toughest fighters in his division. He showcased his skills in notable bouts against renowned boxers such as Paul Banke, Freddie Jackson, and Tracy Harris Patterson.

Zaragoza's journey to becoming a world champion was not an easy one. He faced many challenges and setbacks throughout his career, but his perseverance and unwavering determination always pushed him forward. His fan-favorite fighting style, which combined slick footwork, precision punching, and relentless pressure, captured the hearts of boxing enthusiasts worldwide.

One of Zaragoza's defining moments came in 1988 when he defeated Paul Banke to capture the WBC World Bantamweight title. The fight was an absolute war, with both fighters displaying immense heart and courage. Zaragoza's victory showcased his ability to dig deep and overcome adversity, solidifying his status as a world-class competitor.

Zaragoza's success continued into the 1990s when he moved up to the super bantamweight division. In 1995, he squared off against the talented Tracy Harris Patterson for the WBC Super Bantamweight title. The fight proved to be an epic battle, with Zaragoza showcasing his technical prowess and superb defensive skills. Despite being the underdog, Zaragoza emerged victorious, claiming another world title.

Throughout his reign as a world champion, Zaragoza defended his titles against a slew of top contenders, cementing his legacy as one of the greatest Mexican fighters of his time. His ability to outsmart his opponents inside the ring, coupled with his ironclad chin and unwavering determination, made him a formidable force.

It is worth noting that Zaragoza's boxing journey wasn't limited to his world title wins. He also engaged in several memorable battles that, although not resulting in victory, showcased his incredible skill and determination.

Retiring in 1999, Zaragoza left behind a legacy that will forever be etched in the annals of boxing history. His technical brilliance, relentless style, and ability to overcome adversity endeared him to fans worldwide. Even after his retirement, Zaragoza remains an influential figure in the sport, providing insight and guidance to the upcoming generation of boxers.

Mexican boxers have always had a special place in the hearts of fight fans, and Daniel "El Raton" Zaragoza is no exception. His remarkable career serves as a testament to his unwavering dedication, perseverance, and skill. Zaragoza's name will forever be associated with the golden era of boxing, and his legacy will continue to inspire future generations of fighters to strive for greatness.
