Jake Williams, also known as "Jack Jarrod," is a force to be reckoned with in the boxing world. With a reach of 75 inches (191cm) and a southpaw stance, he possesses the physical attributes that give him a distinct advantage in the ring. Throughout his career, Williams has showcased his skills and left a lasting impression on fans and opponents alike.

Williams' reach of 75 inches (191cm) allows him to keep his opponents at bay and strike from a distance. This provides him with a significant advantage, as he can land powerful blows without getting too close to his opponents, minimizing the chances of a counter-attack. His long arms enable him to connect with precision and force, making him a formidable opponent for anyone who steps foot in the ring with him.

One of Williams' most notable attributes is his southpaw stance, which presents a challenge for orthodox fighters. When facing a southpaw like Williams, who leads with his right hand and uses his left as his power punch, orthodox fighters often find themselves off-balance and at a disadvantage. This gives Williams an edge in terms of strategy and positioning during his bouts.
