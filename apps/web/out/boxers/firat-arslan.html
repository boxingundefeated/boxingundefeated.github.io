Biography

Have you ever heard of Firat Arslan? This seasoned boxer has been in the ring for over two decades, earning a reputation as one of the best cruiserweights of his time. Born on September 28, 1970, Arslan has dedicated his life to the sport and has left an indelible mark in the world of boxing.

Standing at an admirable height of 182cm with a reach of 195cm, Arslan possesses the physical attributes that are essential for success in the ring. His stance as a southpaw adds an element of unpredictability to his fights, making him a difficult opponent to face. Competing in the cruiserweight division, Arslan has established himself as a force to be reckoned with in this weight class.

Throughout his career, Arslan has faced many formidable opponents and showcased his skills in the ring. With over 40 professional bouts under his belt, he has proven time and time again that he is a force to be reckoned with. While he may not have the flashy nickname that some boxers possess, his abilities speak for themselves.

Arslan's journey in boxing has been filled with triumphs and challenges. He has had his fair share of ups and downs, but he has always persevered and come out stronger. This level of dedication and resilience is what sets him apart from other boxers.

One of the highlights of Arslan's career came in 2007 when he faced the reigning World Boxing Association (WBA) cruiserweight champion, Virgil Hill. Despite being considered the underdog, Arslan proved his mettle and secured a stunning victory, becoming the new WBA champion.
