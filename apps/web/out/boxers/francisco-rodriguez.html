Biography

Francisco Rodriguez, also known as "Chihuas," is a talented Mexican boxer who has made a name for himself in the world of professional boxing. Born on July 10, 1993, Rodriguez has proven to be a force to be reckoned with in the ring. Standing at a height of 163cm and sporting an orthodox stance, he has showcased his skill and tenacity in various weight classes.

One of the remarkable aspects of Rodriguez's career is his versatility in different weight classes. He has competed in a wide range of weight divisions, including Minimumweight, Light Flyweight, Flyweight, Super Flyweight, Bantamweight, Super Bantamweight, Featherweight, and Super Featherweight. This demonstrates his ability to adapt and excel in different weight categories, showcasing his dedication and determination to succeed.

Throughout his professional career, Rodriguez has displayed incredible speed, agility, and technical prowess inside the ring. These attributes, combined with his precise punching and defensive skills, have earned him a place among the top boxers in his weight classes.

Rodriguez's journey in the world of professional boxing began with his debut fight in 2009. Since then, he has amassed an impressive record, with numerous victories and notable performances. His ability to outmaneuver opponents and deliver powerful blows has earned him recognition and respect from both fans and fellow boxers.

One of the defining moments in Rodriguez's career occurred on May 31, 2014, when he faced Katsunari Takayama for the IBF Minimumweight title.
