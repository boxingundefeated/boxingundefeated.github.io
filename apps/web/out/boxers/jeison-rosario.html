Biography

Jeison Rosario, also known by his nickname "Banana," is a promising boxer hailing from the Dominican Republic. Born on April 7, 1995, Rosario has quickly made a name for himself in the world of boxing with his impressive skills and determination. Standing at a height of 180cm with a reach of 178cm, Rosario possesses the physical attributes necessary to excel in the ring. As an orthodox fighter, he utilizes his dominant hand and foot to deliver powerful blows while maintaining a solid defensive stance.

Rosario competes in the Middleweight weight class, where his technical prowess and hard-hitting style have earned him significant success. Weighing in at 72.57kg, he strikes fear into his opponents with his relentless aggression and precision striking. His devotion to his craft is evident in his dedication to maintaining his weight and conditioning, ensuring that he is always prepared for any challenge that comes his way.

What sets Rosario apart from other boxers is his uncanny ability to adapt to any situation in the ring. Whether it's analyzing his opponent's weaknesses or adjusting his strategy mid-fight, Rosario demonstrates remarkable versatility and an innate understanding of the sweet science. This adaptability, combined with his explosive power and lightning-quick reflexes, allows him to dictate the pace of the bout and keep his adversaries constantly guessing.

One of Rosario's most notable achievements came on January 18, 2020, when he faced off against Julian Williams for the unified WBA (Super), IBF, and IBO titles. In a stunning upset, Rosario showcased his immense potential by knocking out the heavily favored Williams in the fifth round. This victory catapulted Rosario to the forefront of the Middleweight division and solidified his reputation as a force to be reckoned with.

Despite his meteoric rise, Rosario remains humble and hungry for further success. His work ethic is unmatched, dedicating countless hours to honing his skills and studying his opponents meticulously.
