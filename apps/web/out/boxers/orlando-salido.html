Biography

Orlando Salido, also known by his ring name "Siri," is a Mexican professional boxer who has made a significant impact in the featherweight and super featherweight divisions. Born on November 16, 1980, Salido has established himself as a tenacious and relentless fighter inside the squared circle.

Standing at a height of 168cm, Salido may not be the tallest boxer in his weight classes, but he compensates for it with his outstanding reach, measuring at 170cm. As an orthodox fighter, Salido utilizes his stance effectively to showcase his skills and exploit his opponents' weaknesses.

Salido has competed primarily in the featherweight division, but he has also tested his abilities in the super featherweight class. Known for his durability and ability to absorb punishment, Salido's fighting style often involves pressuring his opponents, wearing them down with consistent aggression.

Throughout his career, Salido has displayed his versatility and determination, leading to some remarkable achievements inside the ring. One of his most notable victories came against Juan Manuel Lopez in 2011, where he won the WBO featherweight title. This intense bout saw Salido engage in an all-out war, eventually stopping Lopez in the eighth round.

Salido's journey has been filled with both triumphs and setbacks, but he has consistently proven himself to be a force to be reckoned with. His dedication to his craft and fearless approach have earned him respect among fans and fellow boxers alike.
