Biography

Jezzrel Corrales, known by his nickname "El Invisible," is a talented boxer who has made waves in the sport since his debut. Born on July 12, 1991, Corrales has proven himself to be a formidable opponent in the ring, consistently showcasing his skills and leaving spectators in awe. Standing at a height of 168cm with a reach of 170cm, he possesses the physical attributes that contribute to his success as a super featherweight boxer.

One of the most intriguing aspects of Corrales' style is his southpaw stance. This means that he leads with his right hand and right foot, a stance favored by many left-handed boxers. It gives him a unique advantage in the ring, as opponents often find it challenging to adjust their strategy to counter his unorthodox style. Corrales utilizes his southpaw stance to unleash powerful punches from unexpected angles, keeping his opponents on their toes and unable to predict his next move.

Corrales competes in the super featherweight weight class, which is known for its fast-paced and action-packed fights. This weight class includes boxers weighing between 130 to 135 pounds. The super featherweight division features some of the most exciting and skillful fighters in the sport, and Corrales has proven that he belongs among them.

With an impressive professional record, Corrales has etched his name in the annals of boxing history. Throughout his career, he has faced formidable opponents and emerged victorious, showcasing his talent, determination, and hunger for success. Corrales continues to strive for greatness, aiming to leave a lasting legacy in the world of boxing.

While his record speaks for itself, Corrales' style is what truly captivates fans and experts alike. He possesses a unique combination of speed, agility, and power, allowing him to dominate his opponents inside the ring. His lightning-fast footwork keeps him one step ahead, while his devastating punches leave opponents reeling.

But it's not just his physical attributes that make Corrales a force to be reckoned with. His mental toughness and strategic approach also play a significant role in his success. Corrales enters each fight with a game plan, analyzing his opponent's weaknesses and capitalizing on them.
