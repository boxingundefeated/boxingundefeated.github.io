Biography

Joshua Clottey, also known as the "Grand Master," is a skilled welterweight boxer who was born on October 6, 1977. With a height of 173cm and a reach of 178cm, he possesses the physical attributes necessary to excel in the ring. A boxer's stance can greatly impact their success in the sport, and Clottey is known for his orthodox stance, which provides stability and balance during his bouts.

One of the key factors in boxing is weight, and Clottey typically competes in the welterweight division. At 71kg, he has found success in this weight class, utilizing his strength and agility to outmaneuver his opponents. This weight class is packed with talent, making it one of the most competitive divisions in boxing.

Throughout his career, Clottey has earned a reputation as a disciplined and focused fighter, always pushing himself to improve and reach new heights in the sport. His nickname, "Grand Master," reflects not only his skill but also his strategic mindset in the ring. Clottey is known for his ability to break down his opponents and capitalize on their weaknesses.

With over 20 years of experience in professional boxing, Clottey has faced some of the sport's top contenders. He has proven himself against formidable opponents, showcasing his talent and determination. Clottey's longevity in the sport speaks to his dedication and love for the sweet science.
