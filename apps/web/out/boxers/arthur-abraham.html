Biography

When it comes to boxing, few fighters have left a lasting impact quite like Arthur Abraham. Known by his nickname "King Arthur," Abraham is a force to be reckoned with in the ring. Born on February 20, 1980, in Yerevan, Armenia, Abraham has had a long and successful career that has seen him compete in multiple weight classes, including Middleweight and Super Middleweight.

Standing at a height of 175cm and boasting a reach of 183cm, Arthur Abraham possesses the physical attributes that make him a formidable opponent. His orthodox stance adds to his ability to deliver powerful punches and counter his opponents effectively. Whether he's trading blows in the middleweight division or showcasing his skills in the super middleweight category, Arthur Abraham has proven time and time again that he is a force to be reckoned with.

Arthur Abraham's career has been filled with memorable moments and significant victories. His tenacious fighting style and unwavering determination have catapulted him to the top of the boxing world. One of Arthur Abraham's defining moments came in 2005 when he claimed the vacant IBF middleweight title by defeating Kingsley Ikeke. This victory not only earned Abraham his first world championship belt but also showcased his ability to dominate his opponents. He successfully defended his title multiple times, solidifying his position as one of the top middleweights in the world.

As Abraham's career progressed, he made the decision to move up in weight class and compete in the super middleweight division. This transition proved to be a wise choice, as he quickly made a name for himself in the new weight class. In 2009, he won the WBC super middleweight title by defeating Jermain Taylor in a keenly contested match.
