Biography

David Carmona, also known as "Severo", is a talented professional boxer who was born on May 1, 1991. Standing at a height of 163cm with a reach of 168cm, Carmona competes in the super flyweight weight class. With an orthodox stance and weighing in at 55.34kg, this Mexican fighter brings an impressive skill set to the ring.

Carmona hails from Mexico, a country with a rich boxing tradition. Growing up in a boxing-loving nation, it comes as no surprise that he developed a passion for the sport at a young age. As he honed his skills and dedication, Carmona made his professional debut on July 29, 2009, against Sergio Ortega. Since then, he has built an impressive record and gained recognition among boxing enthusiasts.

Carmona's boxing style can be described as aggressive and calculated. He possesses excellent hand speed and has developed a reputation for being a fearsome body puncher. His ability to deliver devastating blows to his opponents' midsection often leaves them vulnerable and struggling to recover.

Throughout his professional career, Carmona has faced formidable opponents in the super flyweight division. One notable fight was against Naoya Inoue, a highly skilled Japanese boxer who was considered one of the best in the weight class at the time. In their bout on April 6, 2014, Carmona displayed his resilience and determination, lasting the full 12 rounds against Inoue. Although he ultimately lost the fight by unanimous decision, Carmona showcased his ability to endure and face adversity head-on.

Carmona's record speaks for itself, standing as a testament to his skill and determination. As of now, he boasts an impressive professional record of 34 wins, 7 losses, and 1 draw. Out of his victories, 21 have come by way of knockout, demonstrating his punching power and ability to finish fights decisively.

Throughout his career, Carmona has faced top-ranked opponents, including former world champions and highly regarded contenders. These encounters have provided Carmona with valuable experience and an opportunity to showcase his abilities on a big stage. As he continues to climb the ranks of the super flyweight division, Carmona's name is becoming increasingly recognized.

Carmona's dedication to his craft extends beyond the ring. He is known for his intense training regimens and disciplined approach to his preparation. Training sessions often involve rigorous cardio exercises, strength training, and extensive sparring sessions to sharpen his skills and improve his endurance.

Outside of his training, Carmona also places emphasis on maintaining a healthy lifestyle. Proper nutrition and rest are crucial elements of his routine, allowing him to perform at his best when under the bright lights of the boxing arena.
