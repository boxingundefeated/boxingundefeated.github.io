Biography

When it comes to the world of boxing, there are very few fighters who possess the raw power and skill that Erickson "The Hammer" Lubin brings to the ring. Born on October 1, 1995, Lubin has quickly made a name for himself as one of the most promising super welterweight boxers of his generation. Standing at a height of 180cm and boasting an impressive reach of 189cm, Lubin utilizes his physical attributes to his advantage in every fight.

One of the standout features of Lubin's fighting style is his southpaw stance. This means that he leads with his right hand and has his right foot forward, giving him a unique advantage over orthodox fighters. His left-hand power is legendary, and opponents often find themselves on the receiving end of devastating punches. The combination of his stance, speed, and power make him a formidable opponent for anyone who steps into the ring with him.

When it comes to his weight, Lubin competes in the super welterweight division. Currently weighing in at 69.85kg, he has found success in this weight class and has become a force to be reckoned with. His physique and conditioning allow him to maintain his power and stamina throughout the duration of his fights.

But it's not just his physical attributes that have propelled Lubin to the top of the boxing world. He possesses a natural instinct and ring IQ that is unmatched. Lubin has a deep understanding of the sweet science and knows how to effectively utilize his skills to exploit the weaknesses of his opponents. His ability to adapt and make split-second decisions in the ring is a testament to his years of training and dedication to his craft.

Throughout his career, Lubin has faced numerous challenges and overcome adversity. Every fight is an opportunity for him to showcase his talent and prove himself as one of the best in the business. His journey has not been without setbacks, but each setback has only fueled his desire to succeed and become a world champion.

One of the most memorable moments in Lubin's career came in 2017 when he faced off against Jermell Charlo for the WBC super welterweight title. Despite his youth and relative lack of experience compared to his opponent, Lubin displayed incredible poise and skill in the early rounds of the fight. However, a devastating right hook from Charlo in the first round put an end to Lubin's quest for the title. It was a setback that could have derailed his career, but instead, Lubin used it as motivation to come back stronger than ever.

Since that loss, Lubin has rebounded with a series of impressive victories. He has proven himself as a force to be reckoned with in the super welterweight division, and his stock continues to rise. With each fight, he gains invaluable experience and further hones his skills.

Looking to the future, there is no doubt that Lubin has the potential to become a world champion. His combination of power, skill, and determination make him a formidable opponent for anyone who dares to step into the ring with him. The journey to the top is never an easy one, but Lubin has shown time and time again that he is willing to put in the work and make the necessary sacrifices to achieve greatness. As boxing fans, we can only wait in anticipation to see what lies ahead for Erickson "The Hammer" Lubin.
