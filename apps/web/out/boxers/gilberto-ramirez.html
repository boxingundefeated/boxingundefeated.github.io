Biography

When it comes to boxing, Gilberto Ramirez is a force to be reckoned with. Known by the nickname "Zurdo de Oro" or "Golden Southpaw," Ramirez has earned his place as one of the top super middleweights in the world. With a birthdate of June 19, 1991, Ramirez is still in the prime of his career and has already accomplished impressive feats inside the ring.

Standing tall at 189cm with a reach of 191cm, Ramirez possesses the physical attributes that give him an advantage over his opponents. His southpaw stance adds another layer of complexity to his style, making him a challenging opponent for any fighter in the super middleweight division.

Since turning professional in 2009, Ramirez has been on a path of success. He quickly garnered attention and respect from boxing fans and experts alike with his impressive performances. His skillful boxing abilities combined with his power in the ring make him a formidable opponent.

One of the notable highlights of Ramirez's career was his victory over Arthur Abraham in 2016, where he claimed the WBO super middleweight title. This win made him the first Mexican-born fighter to win a major world title in the super middleweight division. Ramirez showcased his determination and resilience during the fight, earning him praise and recognition as a rising star in the sport.

Throughout his career, Ramirez has faced tough challenges and proven his ability to overcome adversity. He has defended his title successfully multiple times, solidifying his position as a dominant force in the super middleweight division. His exceptional ring craft, footwork, and powerful punches have contributed to his continued success.

What sets Ramirez apart is his relentless work ethic and commitment to his craft. He continually trains to improve his skills and refine his technique, showcasing his dedication to his profession. This level of commitment is what allows him to perform at such a high level and maintain his position as a top-ranked boxer.

As Ramirez continues to make waves in the world of boxing, fans eagerly await his next bout. His exciting style and ability to deliver thrilling fights make him a fan favorite and draw attention from casual viewers and die-hard boxing enthusiasts alike.

Looking ahead, there are several potential matchups that could further solidify Ramirez's legacy in the sport. With his prime years still ahead of him, Ramirez has the potential to take on some of the biggest names in the super middleweight division and add more titles to his already impressive resume.
