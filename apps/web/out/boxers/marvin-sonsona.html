Biography

Marvin Sonsona, born on July 25, 1990, is a Filipino professional boxer known for his exceptional skills in the ring. Standing at a height of 170cm, Sonsona competes in the Super Flyweight weight class, where he has made a name for himself with his extraordinary talent and determination. As a southpaw, Sonsona has the ability to confuse and outmaneuver his opponents, making him a formidable force to be reckoned with in the boxing world.

Throughout his career, Sonsona has showcased his exceptional boxing skills, winning numerous titles and gaining the respect of fans and fellow boxers alike. Although he does not have any official nicknames, his achievements in the ring speak for themselves.

Sonsona burst onto the boxing scene at a young age, turning professional in 2007 at just 17 years old. His professional debut showcased his immense potential and determination, as he defeated his opponent with a TKO in the first round. This electrifying victory set the stage for Sonsona's rise in the boxing world.

One of Sonsona's most notable achievements came in 2009 when he became the WBO World Super Flyweight champion. At the age of 18, Sonsona made history by becoming the youngest Filipino boxer to win a world title. This remarkable feat showcased his exceptional talent and dedication to the sport.

However, Sonsona's career has not been without its setbacks. In 2010, he suffered his first defeat against Wilfredo Vazquez Jr., losing his WBO World Super Flyweight title. Despite this setback, Sonsona demonstrated his resilience and determination by bouncing back and continuing to pursue his dreams in the boxing ring.

Over the course of his career, Sonsona has faced some formidable opponents, including some of the biggest names in the boxing world. His ability to adapt to different styles and come up with effective strategies in the ring has helped him secure victories against tough competitors.
