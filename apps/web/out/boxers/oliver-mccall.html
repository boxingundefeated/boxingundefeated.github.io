Biography

Oliver McCall, also known as "The Atomic Bull," is a legendary figure in the world of boxing. Born on April 21, 1965, McCall made a lasting impact on the sport with his impressive skills and captivating personality. Standing at a towering height of 188cm and possessing a reach of 208cm, he was a formidable force in the heavyweight division.

McCall's orthodox stance and his ability to land powerful punches made him a feared opponent in the ring. As a heavyweight, he competed against some of the best fighters of his time and left a mark with his exceptional performances. Throughout his career, McCall proved to be a persistent and resilient fighter, never backing down from a challenge.

One of the most memorable moments in McCall's career came on November 13, 1994, when he faced the legendary Lennox Lewis for the WBC heavyweight title. The fight took place at Wembley Stadium in London, England, and McCall entered the ring with a determined mindset. In a shocking turn of events, McCall visibly broke down during the bout, crying uncontrollably in the ring. It was a perplexing sight that baffled both the crowd and the commentators. Despite his emotional outburst, McCall managed to secure a second-round knockout victory, becoming the WBC heavyweight champion.

McCall's victory over Lewis propelled him into the spotlight. However, he faced a difficult period after his title win as he battled personal demons and struggled to maintain consistency in his performances. His career was marked by bursts of brilliance and sporadic moments of confusion, which further added to the enigma surrounding him.

One of the most iconic fights in McCall's career came in a rematch against Lennox Lewis on February 7, 1997. The bout, known as "The Rematch of the Cry," was highly anticipated as fans and experts wondered if McCall could replicate his earlier triumph. However, McCall's mental state appeared to be in turmoil once again. Throughout the fight, he exhibited erratic behavior, refusing to throw punches and appearing disoriented.
