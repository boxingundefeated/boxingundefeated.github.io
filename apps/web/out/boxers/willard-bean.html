Biography

Have you ever heard of a boxer with the nickname "Mormon Cyclone"? Well, let me introduce you to Willard Bean, also known as "Parson", a remarkable professional middleweight boxer who made his mark in the early 20th century. Born on May 16, 1868, Willard Bean was a force to be reckoned with in the ring, standing at a height of 175cm and possessing an incredible reach of 74 inches. With his orthodox stance and swift movements, he proved himself to be a true contender in the world of boxing.

One of Willard Bean's most notable attributes was his ability to adapt to his opponents' fighting styles. He possessed a tremendous burstiness in his approach, surprising his adversaries with his quick reflexes and powerful punches. His boxing prowess was not solely dependent on his physical attributes but also on his strategic mindset and determination to succeed.

Willard Bean's career in boxing spanned several decades, making him a highly respected figure in the sport. Competing in the middleweight weight class, he faced off against some of the toughest opponents of his time. Despite his success inside the ring, Bean also led an intriguing life outside of boxing.

Bean's nickname, "Mormon Cyclone," highlights his ties to the Mormon faith. He was a devout member of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, commonly known as the Mormon Church. His faith played a significant role in shaping his character and approach to both boxing and life in general.

Throughout his career, Willard Bean balanced his passion for boxing with his commitment to his religious beliefs. He would self-reflect and dedicate time to study scriptures and attend church services, always seeking guidance and strength from his faith. This unique combination of a pugilist and a parson intrigued and captivated the public, turning him into a well-known and respected figure not only in the boxing world but also within the Mormon community.

As a boxer, Bean's achievements were countless. He had an illustrious career, defeating many formidable opponents and leaving an indelible mark on the sport. On September 19, 1901, he secured the Middleweight Championship of Colorado by defeating Bert Lytell. This victory solidified his place as one of the top middleweight boxers of his era.

However, just as with any athlete, Bean faced his fair share of challenges. In 1909, he suffered a career-threatening injury during a match against "Battling Levinsky." This setback could have sent many boxers into retirement, but not Willard Bean. With unwavering determination and immense perseverance, he made an incredible comeback, demonstrating his resilience and love for the sport.

Willard Bean's boxing career gradually came to an end, but his legacy as a remarkable middleweight boxer lives on. He was a true pioneer in the sport, showcasing not only his extraordinary skills inside the ring but also his ability to overcome adversity and stay true to his faith.

Bean's unique life story makes him an unforgettable figure in the history of boxing. His ability to balance his roles as a fighter and a man of God stood out amongst his peers. Even after retiring from the sport, he continued to inspire others through his unwavering dedication to his faith and community.

Willard Bean's remarkable journey and achievements in the world of boxing have left an enduring impact. He exemplifies the idea that one can pursue their passions while remaining true to their principles. As boxing enthusiasts, let's remember and celebrate the life and career of the "Mormon Cyclone."
