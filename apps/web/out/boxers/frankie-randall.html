Frankie Randall, whose real name is Frankie Warren Randall, is a legendary boxer known for his exceptional skills and technique in the ring. Born on September 25, 1961, Randall has had an illustrious career in the sport, leaving a lasting legacy for future generations to admire.

With a reach of 68 inches (173 cm), Randall possessed the physical attributes that allowed him to effectively control the distance between him and his opponents. His long arms gave him an advantage in delivering powerful punches while keeping his adversaries at bay.

As for his stance, Randall predominantly fought from an orthodox stance, with his left foot forward and his right hand as his power hand. This stance provided him with stability and balance, allowing him to generate maximum power in his punches.

Throughout his career, Frankie Randall competed in two weight classes: Lightweight and Welterweight. He made a name for himself in the Lightweight division, where he faced some of the best fighters of his era.

Randall's career highlights include his victory over Hall of Fame boxer Julio Cesar Chavez in 1994. In what was considered a major upset at the time, Randall defeated Chavez by split decision to claim the WBC World Lightweight title.
