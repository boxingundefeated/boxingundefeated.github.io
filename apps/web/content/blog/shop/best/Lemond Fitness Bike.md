### Safety Considerations

Your safety should be a top priority when choosing a Lemond Fitness bike:

- Sturdiness: Make sure the frame is sturdy and well-built to support your weight during intense workouts.

- Emergency Brake: Some bikes come equipped with an emergency brake feature to quickly stop the pedals in case of an emergency.

- Foot Straps: Ensure the pedals have secure foot straps or clips to keep your feet safely in place during pedaling.

### Maintenance and Durability

Investing in a high-quality Lemond Fitness bike means considering its maintenance requirements and overall durability:

- Material Durability: Look for a bike with a steel or aluminum frame, as these materials are more resistant to corrosion and wear.

- Warranty: Choose a bike with a robust warranty covering defects in materials and workmanship.

- Belt-Driven vs. Chain-Driven: Belt-driven bikes are generally quieter and require less maintenance, while chain-driven bikes can offer more resistance levels but require more frequent lubrication.
