## FAQ

### What is a reclining bike?

A reclining bike, also known as a recumbent bike or semi-recline exercise bike, is a type of stationary exercise bike designed with a reclined seating position, providing more back support and comfort. It features a larger seat, backrest, and foot pedals set further forward than traditional stationary bikes, offering a low-impact workout with reduced strain on joints and muscles.

### How does a reclining bike differ from an upright bike?

The main differences between reclining bikes and upright bikes lie in their design and the benefits offered. While an upright bike follows a traditional cycle seating position, a reclining bike has a reclined seat, providing more back support and comfort. Reclining bikes are generally more comfortable and less strain-inducing, offering a low-impact workout suited for people with joint pain, injuries, or those looking for a more leisurely exercise experience.

### What muscles does a reclining bike work?

A reclining bike primarily works the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and calves, similar to an upright bike. However, due to the unique positioning of the rider and the way the pedals are set further forward, it can also engage the core and upper body muscles to some extent, promoting a well-rounded workout session.
