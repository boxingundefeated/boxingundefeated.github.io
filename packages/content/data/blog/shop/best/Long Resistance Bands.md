## FAQ

### Why use long resistance bands instead of shorter ones?

Long resistance bands are more versatile, providing a larger range of motion and the ability to perform full-body exercises. They are perfect for those who want to target multiple muscle groups in one workout or for those who lack mobility in their joints.

### What exercises can be done with long resistance bands?

- Squats

- Lunges

- Chest presses

- Rowing

- Bicep curls

- Tricep extensions

- Overhead shoulder presses

### How do I choose the right resistance level for me?

Start with a lighter resistance band and work your way up as you get stronger. Aim for a resistance where you can complete at least 10 reps but not more than 15. As you progress, gradually increase the resistance to challenge your muscles further.

### How do I maintain my long resistance bands?

Clean your bands regularly using soap and water or a mild detergent. Store them in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Inspect them for signs of wear and tear regularly, and replace them if needed.

### Are there any safety precautions I should follow when using resistance bands?

- Warm up before using resistance bands.

- Always grip the band securely to prevent slipping.

- Keep tension on the band at all times to prevent sudden snap-backs.

- Perform exercises in a controlled manner to avoid injuries.

### How often should I use long resistance bands for the best results?

To see results, aim to use your long resistance bands 2-3 times per week, incorporating them into your existing workout routine or using them for full-body workouts. Consistency and progressive overload are key factors in achieving results with resistance bands.
