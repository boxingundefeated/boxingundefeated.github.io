### What is a walking bike for adults?

A walking bike, also known as a paddle bike or a draisine, is a human-powered vehicle with two large wheels and a flat board between them for the rider to stand on while pushing the vehicle along with their feet. Unlike a regular bike, a walking bike doesn't have pedals. Instead, you use your feet to push off the ground and propel the bike forward.

### How does a walking bike work?

A walking bike functions by the rider standing on the flat board between the two large wheels and using their feet to push off the ground as if you were walking. As you push down, the walking bike moves forward. Once you're moving, you can maintain speed and balance by swinging your hips or pushing off with the front foot. It takes some practice, but once you get the hang of it, it's quite easy and fun to ride!

### How do I choose the right walking bike for my needs?

Consider the following factors when choosing a walking bike:

1. Size: Make sure the walking bike you choose is the right size for your height and weight. Most models come in various sizes, so find one that fits you comfortably.

2. Wheel size: Walking bikes are available with different wheel sizes. Larger wheels offer more stability and can handle rougher terrain, while smaller wheels are more maneuverable and easier to transport.

3. Frame material: Walking bikes can be made from aluminum, steel, or carbon fiber. Aluminum is lightweight and affordable, while steel is strong and durable. Carbon fiber is lightweight and absorbs vibrations well, but it's typically more expensive.

4. Features: Some walking bikes come with additional features like kickstands, storage racks, and fenders. Decide which features are most important to you and look for a bike that offers them.

### How do I learn to ride a walking bike safely?

Before attempting to ride a walking bike, it's essential to learn proper safety techniques. Here are some tips:

- Wear a helmet and protective gear, such as elbow and knee pads.

- Choose a safe location with minimal traffic, preferably a park or a bike path.

- Ensure the walking bike is properly adjusted to your height and weight.

- Start by standing on the flat board with both feet on the ground for balance. Gradually lift one foot off the ground and push forward while maintaining balance.

- As you begin to move forward, start pushing off with your front foot and swinging your hips to maintain speed and balance.

- Practice turning by shifting your weight to one side, and use your front foot to pivot the walking bike in the desired direction.

- Always be aware of your surroundings and other pedestrians or cyclists when riding.

### How do I maintain and store a walking bike?

Taking care of your walking bike is essential to keep it in good working condition. Here are some tips:

- Keep your walking bike clean by wiping it down with a damp cloth and mild soap after each use.

- Lubricate any moving parts, such as the wheel bearings and the brake system, as needed.

- Store your walking bike in a dry, secure place, like a garage or a shed, when not in use.

- Regularly inspect your walking bike for signs of wear or damage and address any issues promptly. Consult the manufacturer's recommendations for maintenance and repair.
